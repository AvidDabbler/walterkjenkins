About

Walter has been workin in transit and government technology since 2012. He has worked as a voice in government tech to encourage leadership that technology will bridge the gap between its workers and citizens. He belives that through good design barriers of all kinds can be broken down to better communicate projects and initiatives to citizens and elected officials.

Starting in North Carolina Walter worked for a Transportation Authority as a Transportation Planner. He maintained a hand built GTFS feed using GIS and preformed performance and socioeconomic analysis. His methods looking at origin destination data led to a overhaul of the transit system that saw a direct increase in ridership and customer satisfaction.

He has consulted a number of different transit start-ups and advised them on building products. Working with Transloc he used his expertise in building GTFS, GIS and transit operations to spec out a Transloc's web based GTFS builder. He participated in the Remix conference where he showed off his ArcGIS Modelbuilder toolbox with Remix's isochone tool to isolate and calculate census blockgroup level data to compare different scenarios. This methodology is now used within the "Jane" tool in Remix.
